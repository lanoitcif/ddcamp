# 🐉 The Dragon of Whispering Peak

## A DM's Complete Guide to Running the Adventure

**A D&D Adventure for Young Heroes (Ages 8–13)**

> *Sun-Cakes are missing. A dragon is lonely. And your kids are about to become heroes.*

---

**Target audience:** Kids ages 8–13, probably their first D&D session ever
**Duration:** 3–5+ hours of content (you pick what to use — see the Pacing Guide)
**Tone:** Whimsical, heroic, kind-hearted. No gore, no death — characters get **"bonked"** at 0 HP, never killed. Think Studio Ghibli meets D&D.

**How to read this guide:** Each scene has a quick summary, a puzzle breakdown, monster stats, side quests, and DM Tips in callout boxes. You don't need to memorize any of this — skim the scene before you run it and you'll be golden. The VTT engine handles all the dice, HP tracking, and player-facing visuals for you.

---

## 📖 Campaign Overview

The story follows three young heroes — **Lily the Silent** (Rogue), **Brave Thorne** (Fighter), and **Valerius the Just** (Paladin) — as they investigate stolen Sun-Cakes, journey through enchanted woods and crystal caves, and ultimately befriend a lonely dragon named Glint.

There are **12 scenes** organized into **4 chapters**. The campaign is modular — you can run all 12 for an epic session, or cherry-pick scenes for a shorter adventure. Side quests weave between scenes for kids who love exploration.

## 🗺️ Chapter 1: Oakhaven Village

### Scene 1: `bakery` — Mrs. Crumb's Bakery 🧁

*It's the morning of the Summer Festival, and something is very wrong.*

Mrs. Crumb's famous Sun-Cakes — the ones she bakes only once a year — are **GONE!** The display case is empty, the kitchen is a mess, and Mrs. Crumb is in tears. Someone (or some*thing*) raided the bakery overnight.

**🧩 Puzzle: Spotlight Search**
The DM drags a spotlight around the bakery scene. Players call out where to look. Three clues are hidden:

| Clue | Location | What It Means |
|------|----------|---------------|
| Blue Sparkly Scale | Behind the oven | A dragon was here! |
| Golden Crumb Trail | Leading out the door | The thief went toward the woods |
| Mrs. Crumb's Footprint | Near the window | She tried to chase them |

**🗡️ Monsters:** None — this is a mystery scene, not a combat scene.

**📜 Quest:** "Find the Bakery Clue" → Reward: **Golden Crumb**

**🎭 Roleplay — Mrs. Crumb**
Play Mrs. Crumb as tearful but hopeful. She wrings her flour-dusted hands and says things like:
- *"Oh, my beautiful Sun-Cakes! The whole village was counting on me!"*
- *"Please, brave heroes — you're the only ones who can help!"*
- *"I found this strange sparkly scale behind the oven… what could it mean?"*

> **💡 DM Tips**
> - Let each kid search a different area of the bakery. Narrate their discoveries with excitement: *"Lily, your sharp rogue eyes spot something glittering behind the flour sacks…"*
> - Share the **Sun-Cakes handout** image when players find the empty display box — it makes the loss feel real.
> - This scene sets the emotional stakes. Take your time here. The kids need to *care* about the Sun-Cakes before they'll care about the quest.

---

### Scene 2: `market` — Oakhaven Market Square 🏪

*The square buzzes with festival preparation — but trouble is brewing here too.*

The market is alive with colorful stalls, hanging lanterns, and the smell of spiced cider. But a frantic merchant named **Barnaby Barrelsworth** flags down the heroes — his delivery cart was raided overnight, same as the bakery! Something is connecting these thefts.

**🧩 Puzzle: Ingredient Hunt**
Four magical ingredients are scattered around the market. Players drag them into the correct recipe bowls:

| Ingredient | + Ingredient | = Recipe |
|------------|-------------|----------|
| 🫐 Moonberry | ⭐ Starflour | Moon-Bread |
| 🌶️ Dragon Pepper | 🍯 Honey Dew | Sun-Cake |

**🗡️ Monsters:** None — this is a social/exploration scene.

**📜 Side Quests Available:**

| Quest | Description | Reward |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Deliver the Merchant's Parcel** | Barnaby asks the heroes to deliver a package to someone in the woods. (It gets picked up later in `goblin_camp`!) | Pouch of Silver |
| **Find Whiskers the Kitten** | A crying girl named Maisie lost her kitten near the fountain. Skill check **DC 10** to find Whiskers hiding behind barrels. | Lucky Cat Charm |
| **Mrs. Crumb's Secret Recipe** | Mrs. Crumb pulls the heroes aside and whispers: she needs 3 rare ingredients to bake a *legendary* cake. Collect them across the journey: **Moonberries** (glade), **Cave Crystal** (caves), **Star Honey** (camp). | Golden Rolling Pin |

> **💡 DM Tips**
> - This is your **quest hub**. Let the kids wander, talk to NPCs, and pick up side quests. Don't rush them.
> - Use narration to paint the scene: *"You hear a blacksmith hammering, a bard tuning a lute, and somewhere nearby… a kitten meowing?"*
> - The **Secret Recipe** quest spans multiple scenes — award it here, and remind players to look for ingredients as the adventure continues.
> - Barnaby is a jolly, round-bellied merchant with a huge mustache. Play him big and loud.

## 🌲 Chapter 2: The Sparkle Woods

### Scene 3: `woods` — The Sparkle Woods 🌳

*The trees glow emerald green and whisper secrets to anyone who listens.*

The path leads into a forest where every leaf shimmers with faint light. Perched on a low branch is **Hoot the Riddle-Owl** — a plump owl wearing tiny round spectacles. He blocks the path and won't let anyone pass until they answer his riddle.

**🧩 Puzzle: Hoot's Riddle**

> *"I have a heart that doesn't beat,*
> *I have a bed but never sleep,*
> *I can run but have no legs.*
> *What am I?"*

**Answer:** A River 🏞️

**🗡️ Monsters:**

| Monster | HP | Notes |
|---------|----|-------|
| **Hoot the Owl** | 20 | Only fights if the heroes are rude to him! Otherwise, this is a puzzle-only encounter. |
| **Silverfang the Wolf** | 14 | A protective mother wolf guarding her pups. Can be **befriended with food** (use the Snack button as RP) or fought. |

**📜 Quests:**
- "Solve Hoot's Riddle" → **Owl's Blessing**
- "Tame Silverfang" → **Wolf Fang Necklace**

> **💡 DM Tips**
> - Let the kids debate the riddle answer! Give them a minute or two. If they're stuck, use the **Hint button** or have Hoot drop clues: *"Hmmm, you might find my answer if you go fishing…"*
> - Silverfang is a great opportunity for creative problem-solving. If they share a Sun-Cake (or any food), she becomes friendly. She can even become a **companion** for the rest of the journey!
> - Play Hoot as a pompous professor type: *"Ahem! AHEM! One does not simply walk through MY forest without demonstrating adequate intellectual capacity!"*

---

### Scene 4: `glade` — The Fairy Glade 🧚

*Sunbeams pour through the canopy onto a carpet of wildflowers. Something sparkles everywhere.*

The Glade Fairies are in a panic — their enchanted firefly lanterns have **escaped!** Tiny lights zip and dance between the flowers. The Pixie Queen, **Twinkle**, hovers at eye level and pleads for help.

**🧩 Puzzle: Firefly Catch**
The DM releases fireflies one at a time onto the Player View. Players click to catch each firefly before it fades away. Catch all 6 to complete the puzzle!

**🗡️ Monsters:**

| Monster | HP | Notes |
|---------|----|-------|
| **Twinkle the Pixie Queen** | 12 | Mischievous prankster, not evil. Combat is pillow-fight energy. |
| **Sporecap the Sprite** | 10 | Grumpy mushroom creature guarding the glade entrance. Very territorial about his garden. |

**📜 Side Quests:**

| Quest | Description | Reward |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Catch the Escaped Fireflies** | Catch all 6 fireflies in the puzzle | Fairy Lantern |
| **Follow the Mushroom Trail** | Sporecap knows a secret mushroom path. Help tend his garden (RP) and he reveals a shortcut. | Woodland Map |
| 🫐 **Collect Moonberries** | Found in this scene — for Mrs. Crumb's Secret Recipe | *(part of multi-scene quest)* |

> **💡 DM Tips**
> - Twinkle is a prankster — she might turn someone's hair green, make Thorne's sword glow rainbow, or shrink Valerius's shield to the size of a cookie. Play her as *funny*, not threatening.
> - Sporecap speaks in grumpy one-liners: *"FEET OFF THE MUSHROOMS!"* and *"Nobody appreciates a good spore cloud anymore…"*
> - If they help Sporecap's garden, narrate it warmly: *"You carefully pat the soil around the tiny glowing mushrooms. Sporecap watches suspiciously… then his little face breaks into the tiniest smile."*

---

### Scene 5: `stream` — The Glimmer Stream 💧

*A wide stream of liquid silver cuts across the path. Smooth stepping stones glow with embedded crystals.*

The only way forward is across the Glimmer Stream — but not every stone is safe! Some wobble, some sink, and one has a very opinionated frog sitting on it.

**🧩 Puzzle: Stepping Stones**
The stream has **4 rows of 3 stones** each. One stone per row is safe. Players pick their path across. Wrong picks = a hilarious SPLASH (funny, never punishing).

**🗡️ Monsters:** None — this is a puzzle/exploration scene.

**📜 Quest:** "Cross the Glimmer Stream" → **Hero's Badge**

> **💡 DM Tips**
> - Narrate every splash with maximum comedy: *"Thorne steps confidently onto the frog stone and — SPLOOSH! The frog ribbits indignantly, hops onto Thorne's head, and rides him back to shore!"*
> - Each splash is a chance for the other kids to laugh (kindly!) and for the splashed hero to get a fun moment. Nobody gets hurt — just very, very wet.
> - If a kid is getting frustrated, let them make an Athletics or Perception check to spot the safe stone. We want giggles, not tears.

---

### Scene 6: `goblin_camp` — Goblin Hollow ⛺

*Ramshackle tents lean against each other. Crooked lanterns flicker. A smell of burned stew hangs in the air.*

The heroes stumble into a goblin camp — but these goblins aren't what they expected. They're **hungry and scared**, not evil. Their chief, **Grumbletooth**, sits on a log eating a stolen Sun-Cake with an expression of pure guilt. His scout, **Snick**, hides behind a barrel and peeks out nervously.

**🧩 Puzzle: Sneak Path**
If heroes want to sneak through: 4 checkpoints, each with 3 route options. Pick the stealthy path at each point!

**🗡️ Monsters:**

| Monster | HP | Notes |
|---------|----|-------|
| **Grumbletooth** (Goblin Chief) | 25 | Eating a stolen Sun-Cake. Guilty and defensive. |
| **Snick** (Goblin Scout) | 8 | Nervous, twitchy, hides behind things. |

**📜 Side Quests:**

| Quest | Description | Reward |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Negotiate the Goblin Truce** | Offer food or friendship instead of fighting. Share a Sun-Cake or promise to bring food from the village → Grumbletooth becomes an **ally!** | Peace Treaty Scroll |
| **Deliver the Merchant's Parcel** | Barnaby's parcel (from the market quest) is here — the goblins took it! Return it to complete the quest. | Pouch of Silver |

> **💡 DM Tips**
> - ⭐ **This is the BIG moral choice moment.** Fighting works — but diplomacy is *way* more rewarding. This is where kids learn that D&D isn't just about combat.
> - If they choose friendship, Grumbletooth offers to help carry supplies up the mountain. He becomes a goofy, lovable sidekick.
> - Use **❤️ and 🌟 Reactions** to reward kindness. When a kid says "I want to share my food," hit that heart button immediately.
> - Play Grumbletooth as a big softie trying to act tough: *"WE GOBLINS FEAR NOTHING! …please don't take my cake."*
> - Snick is comedy gold: *"Chief! Chief! They have SWORDS! We should run! No wait — they have FOOD! We should stay!"*

## ⛰️ Chapter 3: The Mountain Path

### Scene 7: `caves` — The Crystal Caverns 💎

*Every surface glitters. Crystals line the walls in clusters of blue, pink, gold, and green — and each one hums a musical note when touched.*

The caverns are beautiful but disorienting. Crystal bats flutter overhead, their wings catching the light. Deep in the caves, four large crystals stand in a row, each a different color, each playing a different tone.

**🧩 Puzzle: Crystal Melody (Simon Says)**
The DM plays a sequence of colored crystals (with musical tones). Players must repeat the sequence. The pattern extends each round — complete a **5-note sequence** to win!

**🗡️ Monsters:**

| Monster | HP | Notes |
|---------|----|-------|
| **Crystal Bat Swarm** | 18 | Startled by noise — attacks if players fail the melody puzzle! |

**📜 Side Quests:**

| Quest | Description | Reward |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Learn the Crystal Melody** | Complete the full 5-note sequence | Singing Crystal |
| **Find the Lost Traveler** | Deep in the caves, find old **Finnegan the merchant** who's been lost for weeks, surviving on cave mushrooms. Escort him out! | Traveler's Compass |
| 💎 **Collect Cave Crystal** | Found in this scene — for Mrs. Crumb's Secret Recipe | *(part of multi-scene quest)* |

> **💡 DM Tips**
> - The Crystal Melody is the most "video game-like" puzzle. Kids will love it.
> - If the audio isn't cooperating, **make the sounds with your voice!** *"DING… DONG… DING… BONG!"* The kids will think it's hilarious and it works just as well.
> - Finnegan is a doddering old man who's surprisingly cheerful about being lost: *"Oh, hello! Are you lost too? Wonderful! I've been naming the mushrooms. That one's Gerald."*
> - The Crystal Bat Swarm only attacks on a failed puzzle — give the kids a couple tries before triggering combat.

---

### Scene 8: `bridge` — The Skythread Bridge 🌉

*An ancient rope bridge stretches across a misty chasm. The ropes creak. The planks sway. Far below, you see… nothing but fog.*

The bridge is old and some planks are broken. Worse, a **Shadow Wisp** — a ghostly, translucent creature — drifts beneath the bridge, trailing wisps of silver mist. It blocks the way, but it doesn't look angry. It looks… *sad*.

**🧩 Puzzle:** None — this is a combat/roleplay scene.

**🗡️ Monsters:**

| Monster | HP | Notes |
|---------|----|-------|
| **Shadow Wisp** | 15 | A lonely ghost-like creature. Can be calmed with a campfire story (RP) or defeated in combat. |

**📜 Side Quests:**

| Quest | Description | Reward |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Repair the Skythread Bridge** | Some planks are broken. Heroes make **Athletics checks (DC 10)** to fix them. 3 successful repairs needed. | Builder's Medal |

> **💡 DM Tips**
> - **Build tension!** Describe the bridge swaying, the ropes groaning, the mist swirling below. Make them feel the height (safely — it's all imaginary!).
> - The Shadow Wisp is **SAD, not evil**. It lost its friends long ago and now haunts the bridge out of loneliness. If the heroes tell it a story or show it kindness, it fades away peacefully.
> - Valerius could try a "Lay on Hands" moment (RP) to comfort the Wisp. Narrate it beautifully: *"Your hand glows warm gold, and the Wisp drifts closer… its form softens… and for a moment, it looks like it's smiling."*
> - The bridge repair quest is a nice mechanical challenge — three DC 10 Athletics checks. Let different kids try different planks.

---

### Scene 9: `camp` — Moonlit Campfire 🏕️

*A sheltered clearing under a canopy of stars. Someone built a stone fire ring here long ago. It feels safe. It feels like home.*

This is the **rest scene**. The heroes set up camp, build a fire, and heal their wounds under a sky full of stars. Golden bees drift lazily around the campfire, trailing drops of glowing honey.

**🧩 Puzzle: Star Connect**
Click **7 stars** in the correct order to draw a dragon constellation in the night sky!

**🗡️ Monsters:** None — this scene is sacred. No combat here.

**📜 Side Quests:**

| Quest | Description | Reward |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Share a Hero's Tale** | Each player tells a short story about their character's past. The DM narrates something fun in response. | Story Token |
| **Name a Constellation** | Complete the star puzzle, then name the constellation anything they want! | Star Map |
| 🍯 **Collect Star Honey** | Golden bees visit the campfire — for Mrs. Crumb's Secret Recipe | *(part of multi-scene quest)* |

> **💡 DM Tips**
> - **Heal everyone to full HP!** This is the rest before the final push. Make sure every hero is topped off.
> - Let the kids roleplay around the campfire. Ask open-ended questions:
>   - *"What does your character dream about tonight?"*
>   - *"Lily, you're on first watch. What sounds do you hear in the dark?"*
>   - *"Thorne, you're sharpening your sword by the fire. What are you thinking about?"*
> - Switch to the **calm/bakery mood** for audio. This scene should feel cozy, warm, and safe.
> - If they completed the Goblin Truce, Grumbletooth tells terrible goblin jokes around the fire: *"Why did the goblin cross the road? Because he STOLE the road! Ha! …no? Okay."*
> - The "Name a Constellation" quest is pure creative joy. Accept ANY name. "Steve the Star Dragon" is a valid constellation name.

## 🏔️ Chapter 4: The Summit

### Scene 10: `ruins` — The Forgotten Shrine 🏛️

*Moss-covered pillars rise from cracked stone. An altar sits at the center, covered in ancient runes. The air hums with old magic.*

This shrine has been abandoned for centuries — but its guardian hasn't. When the heroes approach the altar, the **Stone Guardian** awakens. Its eyes blaze blue, and it steps off its pedestal with a thunderous **BOOM**.

**🧩 Puzzle: Rune Match**
A memory game! **4 pairs of ancient runes** are face-down. Flip two at a time to find matches. Complete all 4 pairs to restore the shrine.

**🗡️ Monsters:**

| Monster | HP | Notes |
|---------|----|-------|
| **The Stone Guardian** | 35 | ⚠️ **MINI-BOSS!** The hardest fight in the campaign. Awakens when heroes approach the altar. |

**📜 Quest:** "Restore the Forgotten Shrine" → **Ancient Blessing** (all heroes gain +1 to their next roll — DM tracks manually)

> **💡 DM Tips**
> - Describe the Stone Guardian's awakening dramatically: *"The statue's eyes ignite with blue fire. Stone grinds against stone as it lifts one massive foot… and steps off the pedestal. The ground shakes. It looks at you. And it speaks in a voice like an avalanche: 'WHO… DARES…'"*
> - **Plot twist:** If the heroes complete the Rune Match puzzle *before* engaging in combat, the Guardian becomes **friendly!** It was testing their worthiness, not attacking them. This rewards clever players who explore before fighting.
> - The Stone Guardian is TOUGH at 35 HP. If your group is younger or getting frustrated, have it "crumble" a bit each round: *"Cracks spread across its arm — it's weakening!"*
> - This fight should feel *epic*. Use combat mood audio. Narrate every hit dramatically.

---

### Scene 11: `peak` — Whispering Peak 🐲

*The wind sings. The clouds part. And there, in the mouth of a cave at the very top of the mountain, you see him — a blue dragon, licking frosting off his claws.*

This is it. **Glint the Dragon** — the thief, the final boss, and the loneliest creature on the mountain. He's not scary. He's *scared*. He stole the Sun-Cakes because he was hungry and had no friends. His cave is full of empty cake boxes and crumbs.

**🧩 Puzzle: Dragon's Hoard**
Glint's cave is full of treasures! Sort **6 items** into 3 categories:

| Category | Items to Sort |
|----------|--------------|
| 🏠 Return to Village | Stolen items that belong to the villagers |
| 🐉 Give to Dragon | Shiny things that would make Glint happy |
| 🎒 Keep as Reward | Treasures the heroes have earned |

**🗡️ Monsters:**

| Monster | HP | Notes |
|---------|----|-------|
| **Glint the Dragon** | 45 | 🐉 **FINAL BOSS** — but can be resolved entirely through friendship! |

**📜 Quest:** "Make a New Friend" → **Dragon-Scale Medal**

> **💡 DM Tips**
> - ⭐ **This is the CLIMAX of the entire campaign.** Take your time. Make it matter.
>
> - **🤝 Friendship Path:** Heroes share food, talk to Glint, ask why he took the cakes. Use **❤️ and 🌟 reactions** generously. Glint admits he was lonely — he saw the village celebrating and wanted to be part of it. He starts crying (big, dragon-sized tears). The heroes invite him back to the village. Use the **Snack button** for dramatic "offering food to the dragon" moments.
>
> - **⚔️ Combat Path:** If they fight, Glint fights back — but he **surrenders at 15 HP** and starts crying. Even if combat happens, the scene should end with friendship. *"Glint drops to the ground, curls his tail around himself, and whispers: 'I just… I just wanted a friend…'"*
>
> - **Either way, the answer is friendship.** This is the core message of the campaign: every problem has a kind solution.
>
> - Play Glint as a big, clumsy puppy in dragon form: *"Oh no, you found me! Please don't be mad! The cakes were just SO good and I was SO hungry and — wait, is that a Sun-Cake? Can I have one? Please? PLEASE?"*

---

### Scene 12: `celebration` — Festival of Heroes 🎉

*Back in Oakhaven! The village square is transformed with bunting, music, and the smell of fresh Sun-Cakes. Everyone is cheering. And there — in a tiny chef hat — is Glint, helping Mrs. Crumb at the bakery.*

**This scene is pure celebration.** No puzzles, no monsters, no danger. Just joy.

Mrs. Crumb renamed her bakery **"The Dragon & The Cake"** in honor of Glint. The whole village turns out to celebrate the heroes. Fireworks (well, fairy-lights) fill the sky.

**🧩 Puzzle:** None — this is the victory lap!

**🗡️ Monsters:** None — only friends here.

**📜 Final Rewards:**
- Share the **Dragon-Scale Medal** handout
- Share the **Free Sun-Cake Pass** handout
- Fire off **🎉 Reactions** on the Player View!
- If they completed Mrs. Crumb's Secret Recipe quest, she bakes a **LEGENDARY CAKE** and everyone gets a slice (narrate this with maximum fanfare)
- Award **every side quest reward** they earned throughout the journey

> **💡 DM Tips**
> - Ask each kid: *"What does your character do at the celebration?"*
> - Let them describe their **victory dance**. Narrate the crowd's reaction.
> - If Grumbletooth came along, he's trying to learn to bake. Badly. *"Is the flour supposed to be on fire?"*
> - If they befriended Silverfang, the wolf pups are playing with Whiskers the kitten.
> - This scene is short but important — it gives **closure**. Every hero should feel like a legend.
> - End with something like: *"And so, the heroes of Oakhaven saved the Sun-Cakes, made a new friend, and proved that the bravest thing you can do… is be kind. The End."*

## ⏱️ DM Pacing Guide

Not every group needs all 12 scenes. Pick the path that fits your time!

| Time Budget | Scenes to Use | Style |
|-------------|---------------|-------|
| **1 hour** (quick demo) | `bakery` → `woods` → `peak` → `celebration` | Skip side quests, fast RP, get to the dragon! |
| **2 hours** (standard session) | `bakery` → `market` → `woods` → `stream` → `goblin_camp` → `peak` → `celebration` | A few side quests, moderate RP |
| **3 hours** (full campaign) | All 12 scenes in order | All side quests, full roleplay at every scene |
| **4+ hours** (epic marathon) | All 12 scenes + extended RP + multiple combats per scene | Every side quest, elaborate RP, let kids fully explore |

**General pacing tips:**
- If energy is high, lean into roleplay and side quests — let them explore.
- If energy is flagging, skip to the next scene. You can always come back.
- The `camp` scene is a natural "halftime break" — use it for snacks and bathroom trips IRL too.
- Never rush the `peak` or `celebration` scenes. Those are the payoff.

## 🎵 Atmosphere & Audio

Each scene has unique procedural audio (Minecraft-style ambient generation). The DM can also switch mood within a scene using the audio controls.

| Scene | Audio Mood | Vibe |
|-------|-----------|------|
| `bakery` / `market` | Warm golden pads | Cozy village morning, bustling and safe |
| `woods` / `glade` / `stream` | Ethereal emerald shimmer | Enchanted forest, wonder and mystery |
| `goblin_camp` | Tense mischievous minor key | Sneaky, uncertain, a little bit funny |
| `caves` | Crystalline echoes | Underground beauty, musical and strange |
| `bridge` | Airy exposed wind | Vertigo, openness, lonely |
| `camp` | Cozy nighttime lullaby | Warm, safe, starlit, peaceful |
| `ruins` | Ancient mysterious tones | Old magic, reverence, power |
| `peak` | Singing wind and bells | Climactic, emotional, majestic |
| `celebration` | Bright and joyful | PARTY TIME! 🎉 |

**Mood switches available per scene:** Calm → Tense → Combat. Use the DM Console audio controls to shift moods as the scene demands.

## 🎭 Roleplay Prompts for Each Character

Use these to spark RP if the kids are quiet or unsure what to do. Pick one that fits the current scene!

### Lily the Silent (Rogue) 🗡️
- *"Lily, you notice something shiny under that barrel. What do you do?"*
- *"Your thief instincts tingle — someone is watching from the shadows. Do you investigate?"*
- *"You find a locked chest! Do you try to pick the lock?"*
- *"You spot tracks in the mud that nobody else noticed. Where do they lead?"*
- *"A merchant left his coin purse unattended. What does Lily do?"* (test their heroic instincts!)

### Brave Thorne (Fighter) ⚔️
- *"Thorne, you see the path is blocked by a fallen tree. How do you handle it?"*
- *"Your sword hums — it senses danger ahead. Do you draw it?"*
- *"A younger kid in the village looks scared. What does the brave fighter do?"*
- *"You hear a roar in the distance. Thorne, how do you react?"*
- *"The bridge looks weak. Do you go first to test it for your friends?"*

### Valerius the Just (Paladin) ✨
- *"Valerius, you feel a warm glow in your hand — your holy magic senses something nearby."*
- *"A wounded animal lies on the path. Do you use your healing touch?"*
- *"The shrine calls to you. What prayer do you offer?"*
- *"Someone is being unkind to a smaller creature. What does the paladin of justice do?"*
- *"You can feel that this place was once sacred. What does Valerius sense about its history?"*

> **💡 General RP Tips**
> - Always phrase prompts as questions, not commands. *"What do you do?"* is the magic phrase.
> - If a kid says something unexpected, say **"Yes, and…"** and build on it.
> - Reward creative solutions with Reactions (❤️, 🌟, 🎉) — kids light up when the TV reacts to their choices.
> - If a kid is very quiet, give them a low-pressure prompt: *"Lily, your character notices a butterfly. What color is it?"* Sometimes small moments open the door.
