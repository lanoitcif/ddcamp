# The Dragon of Whispering Peak
A D&D Adventure for Young Heroes (Ages 8-13)

**Tone:** Classic High Fantasy (Whimsical, Heroic, Kind-hearted)
**Setting:** The peaceful village of Oakhaven and the nearby Whispering Peak.

---

## Adventure Overview
The village of Oakhaven is famous for its "Sun-Cakes," but they have mysteriously vanished! The players must track the thief through the magical Sparkle Woods and climb the Whispering Peak to confront a legendary dragon.

### Simple Mechanics for Kids
- **The D20 Rule:** For most actions (climbing, searching, talking), roll a 20-sided die. 
    - **10+** is a "Success."
    - **15+** is a "Great Success!"
- **Help Action:** If one player helps another, the roller gets to roll two dice and pick the highest (Advantage).
- **No "Dying":** If a player runs out of health, they are "Tired" or "Bonked" and can be cheered up by a friend or a snack.

## Act 1: The Missing Sun-Cakes
**Location:** The Village of Oakhaven

The adventure begins on the morning of the Summer Festival. The air smells like honey and cinnamon—until a scream rings out from the bakery!

### NPC: Mrs. Crumb (The Baker)
*A stout, friendly halfling with flour on her nose and a very large rolling pin.*
- **The Problem:** Her tray of "Sun-Cakes" (golden, glowing pastries that make you feel warm inside) has been stolen right off the windowsill.
- **The Clue:** There are blue, sparkly scales stuck to the window frame and a trail of golden crumbs leading toward the **Sparkle Woods**.

**Player Goal:** Interview Mrs. Crumb and follow the crumb trail.
- *Investigation Check:* Finding a small, bright blue scale that feels warm to the touch.

---

## Act 2: The Sparkle Woods
**Location:** A forest where the leaves glow like emeralds and the trees whisper secrets.

### Encounter 1: Hoot the Riddle-Owl
A giant owl with spectacles named **Hoot** blocks the path. He is the guardian of the woods.
- **The Challenge:** Hoot won't let them pass unless they solve his favorite riddle.
- **The Riddle:** *"I have a heart that doesn't beat, I have a bed but never sleep, I can run but have no legs. What am I?"*
- **Answer:** A River. (If the kids get stuck, Hoot gives them a hint: "I'm very wet!")

### Encounter 2: The Glimmer Stream
The path is cut off by a rushing stream of liquid silver. 
- **The Challenge:** The bridge is broken. 
- **Mechanics:** 
    - **Athletics:** Jump across the stones (Roll 10+).
    - **Creative:** Use a fallen log or magic to cross.
- **The Reward:** On the other side, they find a half-eaten Sun-Cake! The thief is close.

---

## Act 3: Whispering Peak
**Location:** A tall mountain where the wind sounds like soft singing.

The trail leads to a cozy cave at the very top. Inside, the players don't find a pile of gold, but a pile of... soft blankets and empty pastry boxes.

### Encounter: Glint the Dragon
**Glint** is a Small-to-Medium Blue Dragon. He isn't scary; he looks embarrassed. He is currently trying to lick the last bits of frosting off his claws.
- **Personality:** Glint is a "Teenage" dragon. He isn't evil, just very hungry and lonely. He smelled the Sun-Cakes from the mountain and couldn't help himself.
- **The Conflict:** Glint is sad because he thinks everyone hates dragons. He stole the cakes because he wanted to taste "sunlight."

### Resolution Options
1. **The Diplomatic Path:** Players convince Glint to apologize. Maybe he can use his dragon-breath to help bake the cakes faster in exchange for a fair share?
2. **The Heroic Path:** Players trade their own trail rations (or a shiny trinket) for the remaining cakes.
3. **The Fun Path:** Invite Glint to the festival as the guest of honor!

---

## Conclusion
The heroes return to Oakhaven with Glint (and hopefully some cakes). Mrs. Crumb is so happy she names the bakery **"The Dragon & The Cake."** 

**Rewards:**
- A "Dragon-Scale Medal" from the Mayor.
- A permanent "Free Sun-Cake" pass.
- A new dragon friend who can fly them around the valley!
